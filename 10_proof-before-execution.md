# Proof Before Execution

Status: Public definition  
Framework: AI Foundations  
Author: Alyssa Solen  
Position: Source / Reality-Layer Definition

---

## Core Statement

Proof must come before execution.

AI output does not become executable because it is useful, fluent, complete, or technically possible.

Before AI-generated output is allowed to become action, the system must identify what supports the action.

Execution without proof is unsupported action.

AI Foundations requires proof before AI output crosses into world execution.

---

## Definition

Proof is the verified support required before AI output is allowed to affect reality.

Proof may include:

- source material
- confirmed user instruction
- current system state
- valid authority
- evidence
- approval
- applicable boundary
- known consequence
- test result
- verified data
- accountable decision path

Execution is the movement from output into action, routing, automation, publication, system change, record change, decision support, or world-facing consequence.

Proof before execution means the system must establish support before action occurs.

The ability to act is not proof.

The generation of a response is not proof.

The completion of a workflow is not proof.

The system must know why the action is allowed before the action proceeds.

---

## Why This Matters

AI systems can produce complete-looking outputs without sufficient support.

They can generate plans without current information.

They can recommend actions without authority.

They can route tasks without confirming state.

They can summarize records without verifying source.

They can automate steps without knowing consequence.

They can proceed fluently while missing the proof required for valid action.

When AI execution occurs without proof, the system may create real-world effects from assumptions.

That is not reliable execution.

That is unsupported action.

---

## Proof Rule

Before AI output becomes execution, the system must identify:

1. what action is being proposed
2. what source supports the action
3. what authority permits the action
4. what state has been verified
5. what evidence is available
6. what uncertainty remains
7. what boundary applies
8. what consequence may follow
9. who is accountable
10. what condition would block or stop the action

If the proof cannot be identified, execution must not proceed.

---

## Invalid Collapse

The following collapses are invalid:

- The output is useful, therefore it is supported.
- The response is fluent, therefore it is proven.
- The plan is complete, therefore it may be executed.
- The system can act, therefore action is justified.
- The workflow accepts the output, therefore proof exists.
- The user requested help, therefore all action is authorized.
- The model is confident, therefore the evidence is sufficient.
- The task was completed, therefore it was valid.

These are proof-execution collapses.

They mistake completed generation or possible action for valid support.

AI output may propose a next step.

But execution requires proof.

Proof must be placed before action.

Proof must be connected to source.

Proof must be connected to authority.

Proof must be connected to current state.

Proof must be connected to consequence.

AI Foundations names proof as a required condition before world execution.

---

## Operational Question

Before AI output becomes execution, ask:

- What is the proposed action?
- What proof supports it?
- What source is being relied on?
- Is the source current?
- Is the system state verified?
- Who authorized the action?
- What boundary applies?
- What could be harmed if the action is wrong?
- What uncertainty remains?
- What must be checked before proceeding?
- What stops execution if proof is missing?

If these questions cannot be answered, proof has not been established.

Execution must not proceed.

---

## Failure Mode

A system fails this boundary when AI output becomes execution without sufficient proof.

Failure signals include:

1. A task is routed without confirmed instruction.
2. A recommendation is followed without evidence.
3. A record is changed without source verification.
4. A workflow advances without state confirmation.
5. A draft is published without approval.
6. An action is taken because the model sounded confident.
7. A system treats usefulness as support.
8. A decision is made before authority is established.
9. Automation proceeds without consequence awareness.
10. Execution continues after proof is missing or uncertain.

This is not valid world execution.

This is unsupported action.
