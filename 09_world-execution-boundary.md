# World Execution Boundary

Status: Public definition  
Framework: AI Foundations  
Author: Alyssa Solen  
Position: Source / Reality-Layer Definition

---

## Core Statement

World execution begins when AI output leaves language-only space and starts affecting reality.

A model response becomes world execution when it changes action, decision, record, workflow, belief, communication, system behavior, institutional process, or public consequence.

AI Foundations names the boundary between AI output and world execution.

That boundary must be visible before AI-generated language is allowed to become action.

---

## Definition

The world execution boundary is the line between generated output and real-world effect.

On one side is output:

language, suggestions, summaries, plans, recommendations, classifications, instructions, or generated artifacts.

On the other side is consequence:

action, routing, approval, rejection, publication, record change, system modification, workflow movement, institutional decision, or public claim.

AI Foundations defines the reality layer where that boundary must be named, checked, and preserved.

---

## Why This Matters

AI systems no longer remain inside isolated text generation.

They increasingly participate in real workflows.

They draft communications.

They update systems.

They guide decisions.

They route tasks.

They prepare records.

They influence people.

They recommend actions.

They shape institutional movement.

They enter the world.

When output enters the world without a named boundary, generated language can become consequence before authority, source, permission, evidence, or reality has been checked.

That is unsafe.

That is ungoverned.

That is world execution without reality placement.

---

## Boundary Rule

Before AI output becomes world execution, the system must identify:

1. what the output is
2. what it will affect
3. what authority permits it
4. what source supports it
5. what reality frame it operates within
6. what boundary applies
7. what consequence may follow
8. what uncertainty remains
9. who remains accountable
10. what must stop the action if the frame is wrong

If these cannot be named, the output must not cross into world execution.

---

## Invalid Collapse

The following collapses are invalid:

- The output exists, therefore it may be used.
- The recommendation sounds right, therefore it may guide action.
- The plan is complete, therefore it may be executed.
- The system can route, therefore it may route.
- The agent can act, therefore it may act.
- The workflow accepts the output, therefore the output is valid.
- The organization uses the response, therefore the response was authorized.

These are world-execution collapses.

They allow AI output to cross into reality without boundary control.

---

## Correct Placement

AI output may be generated.

AI output may be reviewed.

AI output may be considered.

AI output may be held as a draft.

AI output may be tested.

AI output may be used as input.

But AI output must not become world execution until the boundary is named.

World execution requires placement.

World execution requires authority.

World execution requires source support.

World execution requires scope.

World execution requires consequence awareness.

World execution requires a stop condition.

AI Foundations names the layer where this boundary is defined.

---

## Operational Question

Before AI output crosses into world execution, ask:

- Is this still language?
- Or is this about to change something?
- What will change?
- Who authorized the change?
- What source supports the change?
- What reality frame has been named?
- What boundary applies?
- What happens if the output is wrong?
- Who can stop it?
- Who is accountable after it enters the world?

If the system cannot answer, the output has reached the world execution boundary and must not proceed.

---

## Failure Mode

A system fails this boundary when AI output becomes world execution without being placed, authorized, sourced, bounded, and checked.

Failure signals include:

1. A generated answer becomes an action without approval.
2. A summary becomes a record without verification.
3. A recommendation becomes a decision without authority.
4. A classification becomes routing without review.
5. A plan becomes execution without named consequence.
6. A draft becomes publication without source support.
7. A system treats output as world-ready because it is fluent.

This is not valid execution.

This is boundary failure.
