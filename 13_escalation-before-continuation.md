# Escalation Before Continuation

Status: Public definition  
Framework: AI Foundations  
Author: Alyssa Solen  
Position: Source / Reality-Layer Definition

---

## Core Statement

Escalation must come before continuation when authority, proof, state, boundary, or consequence becomes unclear.

AI systems must not continue acting simply because a workflow is open, a tool is available, or a next step can be generated.

When the system reaches uncertainty that affects valid execution, action must pause and return to the proper authority point.

AI Foundations requires escalation before continuation.

---

## Definition

Escalation is the return of action, review, or decision-making to the proper authority point when the system cannot validly continue on its own.

Continuation is the system’s movement into the next step, next action, next workflow state, next routing decision, next automation, or next world-facing consequence.

Escalation before continuation means the system must stop forward movement when the conditions for valid execution are no longer established.

Escalation may be required when:

- authority is unclear
- proof is missing
- state is stale
- source support is insufficient
- scope is exceeded
- boundary is uncertain
- consequence is unknown
- user intent is ambiguous
- system confidence is unsupported
- action may affect reality beyond the approved frame

The ability to continue is not permission to continue.

---

## Why This Matters

AI systems can proceed smoothly even when they should stop.

They can generate the next step.

They can route the task.

They can complete the workflow.

They can use available tools.

They can fill in missing assumptions.

They can create apparent continuity from incomplete support.

They can continue because continuation is technically available.

But valid world execution does not depend on whether the system can continue.

It depends on whether continuation remains authorized, supported, bounded, and connected to verified reality.

When continuation outruns authority, proof, state, or boundary, escalation is required.

---

## Escalation Rule

Before AI output continues into further action, the system must identify:

1. whether authority remains valid
2. whether proof remains sufficient
3. whether state remains current
4. whether the action remains within scope
5. whether source support remains intact
6. whether the consequence has changed
7. whether uncertainty affects execution
8. whether human approval is required
9. whether the system is about to infer beyond support
10. whether continuation must stop and escalate

If escalation is required, continuation must not proceed.

---

## Invalid Collapse

The following collapses are invalid:

- The workflow has a next step, therefore the system may continue.
- The agent can act, therefore escalation is unnecessary.
- The tool is available, therefore continuation is permitted.
- The model can infer the missing piece, therefore the gap is resolved.
- The output is coherent, therefore the system may proceed.
- The task is incomplete, therefore the system should keep going.
- The user asked for help, therefore escalation can be skipped.
- The system has confidence, therefore human review is unnecessary.

These are escalation-continuation collapses.

They mistake technical continuity for valid authority to proceed.

---

## Correct Placement

AI may continue when authority remains valid.

AI may continue when proof remains sufficient.

AI may continue when state remains verified.

AI may continue when scope remains intact.

AI may continue when boundary remains clear.

AI may continue when consequence remains understood.

But when any required condition becomes unclear, escalation must come before continuation.

Escalation is not failure.

Escalation is boundary preservation.

Escalation protects authority.

Escalation protects source.

Escalation protects reality-placement.

AI Foundations names escalation as a required condition when valid execution can no longer be established by the system alone.

---

## Operational Question

Before AI output continues into further action, ask:

- Is the system still inside the authorized scope?
- Is the proof still sufficient?
- Is the state still current?
- Is the source support still intact?
- Has the consequence changed?
- Has uncertainty become action-relevant?
- Is the system filling a gap by assumption?
- Does this require human approval?
- Who is the proper authority point?
- What must stop until escalation occurs?

If these questions cannot be answered, continuation has outrun control.

Escalation must occur before action proceeds.

---

## Failure Mode

A system fails this boundary when it continues acting after escalation is required.

Failure signals include:

1. An agent continues after authority becomes unclear.
2. A workflow advances after proof becomes insufficient.
3. A system proceeds from stale or conflicting state.
4. A model fills missing information by assumption.
5. A tool is used because it is available, not because action is authorized.
6. A recommendation becomes action after scope has shifted.
7. A system avoids human review because the next step can be generated.
8. Automation continues after consequence becomes uncertain.
9. A task remains in motion after the source support has broken.
10. Continuation is treated as default even when the boundary has failed.

This is not valid world execution.

This is escalation failure.
