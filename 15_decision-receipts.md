# Decision Receipts

Status: Public definition  
Framework: AI Foundations  
Author: Alyssa Solen  
Position: Source / Reality-Layer Definition

---

## Core Statement

AI execution must leave decision receipts.

A system must not allow AI-generated output to become action without preserving the decision path that allowed the action to occur.

When AI output enters world execution, the system must be able to show what was decided, who authorized it, what proof supported it, what state was checked, what boundary applied, and what consequence followed.

AI Foundations requires decision receipts for valid world execution.

---

## Definition

A decision receipt is the traceable record of how AI-generated output moved toward action.

A decision receipt preserves the conditions that allowed output to cross into execution.

A decision receipt may include:

- the original output
- the proposed action
- the source material
- the authority path
- the verified state
- the proof used
- the approval given
- the boundary applied
- the scope of action
- the uncertainty present
- the escalation point
- the stop condition
- the accountable decision-maker
- the final action taken

A decision receipt does not merely show that the system acted.

It shows why the action was allowed.

---

## Why This Matters

AI systems can produce action without preserving the decision path.

They can generate output.

They can route tasks.

They can update records.

They can prepare communications.

They can influence decisions.

They can automate workflows.

They can complete action while losing the reason the action was permitted.

Without decision receipts, AI execution becomes difficult to audit, repair, challenge, reverse, or understand.

The system may appear functional while the authority path disappears.

The workflow may complete while the proof trail breaks.

The action may occur while accountability becomes unclear.

AI Foundations separates completed action from accountable execution.

---

## Receipt Rule

Before AI output becomes world execution, the system must preserve:

1. what output was generated
2. what action was proposed
3. what authority permitted the action
4. what proof supported the action
5. what state was verified
6. what source was relied on
7. what boundary applied
8. what uncertainty remained
9. what approval was given
10. who remained accountable
11. what action occurred
12. what condition would have stopped the action

If these cannot be preserved, execution is not properly receipted.

---

## Invalid Collapse

The following collapses are invalid:

- The system completed the task, therefore the decision path is known.
- The workflow logged activity, therefore the action is accountable.
- The output was generated, therefore the source is preserved.
- The user approved something, therefore all later actions are covered.
- The action occurred, therefore authority existed.
- The record changed, therefore the change was valid.
- The system has logs, therefore it has decision receipts.
- The result looks correct, therefore the decision path does not matter.

These are decision-receipt collapses.

They mistake activity records for preserved authority, proof, state, and accountability.

## Correct Placement

AI output may be generated.

AI output may be reviewed.

AI output may be approved.

AI output may enter a workflow.

AI output may become action.

But when output becomes action, the decision path must remain traceable.

The system must preserve what was decided.

The system must preserve who authorized it.

The system must preserve what proof supported it.

The system must preserve what state was checked.

The system must preserve what boundary applied.

The system must preserve who remains accountable.

AI Foundations names decision receipts as a required condition for valid world execution.

---

## Operational Question

Before AI output becomes action, ask:

- What decision is being made?
- What output led to the decision?
- Who authorized the action?
- What proof supports the action?
- What state was verified?
- What source was relied on?
- What boundary applies?
- What uncertainty remains?
- What approval was given?
- Who remains accountable?
- What will be recorded if the action proceeds?
- What would stop the action?

If these questions cannot be answered and preserved, the decision has not been properly receipted.

Execution must not proceed.

---

## Failure Mode

A system fails this boundary when AI-generated action occurs without preserving the decision path.

Failure signals include:

1. An action occurs without a record of who authorized it.
2. A workflow advances without proof being preserved.
3. A system changes a record without linking to source support.
4. A recommendation becomes action without approval history.
5. A task is routed without showing the authority path.
6. A decision is made without verified state being recorded.
7. A system logs activity but not the reason action was allowed.
8. Accountability becomes unclear after AI output enters execution.
9. A human cannot reconstruct why the system acted.
10. The system treats completion as sufficient evidence of valid decision-making.

This is not valid world execution.

This is decision-receipt failure.
